Wheat Grain Products

Wheat is one of the most widely consumed and cultivated cereal grains across the world. Its vast culinary applications and numerous health benefits make it a preferred staple globally. Whole wheat grains are naturally low in fat, high in fiber, and rich in carbohydrates. They are also an excellent source of essential nutrients such as B vitamins, vitamin E, phytochemicals, and various minerals that support overall health.

  • Milling Grade Wheat Grains – Used for flour production
  • Hard Red Wheat Grains – High in protein and ideal for bread and baked goods
  • White Grade Wheat Grains – Softer variety preferred for pastries and confectionery
  • Feed Grade Wheat Grains – Suitable for livestock feed and agricultural use

Sorghum (Milo)

Sorghum, also known as milo or great millet, is a highly valued grain rich in protein and fiber. Whole grain sorghum can be incorporated into cooked dishes, snacks, salads, or even ground into flour to create roti and other staple foods. This gluten-free cereal grain is abundant in antioxidants and phytochemicals, offering numerous health benefits.

Millets

Millets are small-seeded grains that are considered nutritionally superior to widely consumed cereals like rice and wheat. These grains are rich in fiber, protein, essential vitamins, minerals, and amino acids, making them a powerhouse of nutrition. Millets can be consumed in whole grain form or ground into flour to prepare various dishes, including roti, porridge, and healthy snacks.

Peanut Kernels

Peanut kernels, though often referred to as nuts, are actually legumes related to peas, lentils, and chickpeas. Peanuts grow underground as part of a unique flowering process, maturing within their brown, patterned shells that typically contain two or three kernels. Each oval-shaped kernel consists of two off-white lobes covered by a brownish-red skin. Peanuts are the leading oilseed crop in India, contributing to nearly 25% of the country’s total oilseed production.
